The molecule is a nucleotide conjugate that is produced by Agrobacterium radiobacter strain K-84. A 'Trojan horse' antibiotic used to control crown gall. It has a role as a bacterial metabolite, an EC 6.1.1.4 (leucine--tRNA ligase) inhibitor and an antimicrobial agent. It is a monosaccharide derivative, a phosphoramidate ester and a nucleotide conjugate. It derives from a D-galactose and an adenosine 5'-monophosphate.
