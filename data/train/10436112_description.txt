The molecule is a class I yanuthone that is yanuthone E in which the secondary hydroxy group has been oxidised to the corresponding ketone. It is the only one of the sixteen yanuthones described up to 2015 to display strong antimicrobial activity. It has a role as an Aspergillus metabolite and an antifungal agent. It is a class I yanuthone, a dicarboxylic acid monoester and a tertiary alcohol. It derives from a yanuthone E and a 3-hydroxy-3-methylglutaric acid.
