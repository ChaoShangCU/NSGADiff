The molecule is an organic heterotricyclic compound and tetramic acid derivative isolated from a mutant strain of Saccharothrix syringae. Nocamycin E exhibits considerably lower antibacterial activity than the corresponding methyl ester, nocamycin I. It has a role as a bacterial metabolite. It is a gamma-lactam, an enol, a monocarboxylic acid, an olefinic compound, an organic heterotricyclic compound, a spiroketal and a member of tetramic acids. It is a conjugate acid of a nocamycin E(2-).
