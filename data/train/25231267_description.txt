The molecule is a dihydroxyflavanone that is (2S)-flavanone substituted by hydroxy groups at positions 5 and 2', a methoxy group at position 7, a prenyl group at position 8 and a 6,6-dimethyl-3,6-dihydro-2H-pyran ring fused across positions 4' and 5'. Isolated from the stem barks of Maackia amurensis, it exhibits cytotoxicity against human cancer cell lines. It has a role as a metabolite and an antineoplastic agent. It is a dihydroxyflavanone and a monomethoxyflavanone.
