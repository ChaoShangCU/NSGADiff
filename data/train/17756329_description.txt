The molecule is a furofuran that is hexahydrofuro[3,2-b]furan which is substituted at position 2 by a (1E,3E)-4-bromohexa-1,3-dien-1-yl group and at position 5 by a (1R)-3-bromopropadienyl group (the 2R,3aS,5R,6aS diastereoisomer). A natural product first isolated from the red alga Laurencia okamurai Yamada and subsequently found in the sea hare Aplysia kurodai, aplysiallene is an inhibitor of sodium-potassium adenosine triphosphatase. It has a role as a metabolite and an EC 3.6.3.9 (Na(+)/K(+)-transporting ATPase) inhibitor. It is an organobromine compound, a member of allenes and a furofuran.
